What makes you a High Risk Driver

There are some factors that consistently qualify you as a high-risk driver. Having any of these marks on your record could boost your insurance rates or disqualify you for better insurance.

Speeding tickets


One of hte biggest culprits in high-risk driving is speeding tickets. if you have multiple speeding tickets, it shows a disregard for even the simplest traffic rules

At-fault Accidents


A signle traffic violation can make you a high risk. If you are at fault for an accident, your actions are in some way responsible for causing the accident to occur.

Impaired Driving


Another huge red flag for high-risk driving is impaired driving, expecially DUIs or DWIs

Careless or Stunt Driving


To insurance companies, stunt driving says you not only dont care about traffic laws, but you're also willing to disregard them to put others at risk from your carelessness.

Non-Payment Cancellation


if you've had a policy canceled before due to non-payment, that's a warning sign to future insurers that you may do the same thing to them.

High-Risk Vehicle


Insurance companies predict which cars are more or less safe than others by looking at how they're configured.

No Prior Insurance Experience


Having no prior insurance experience is sort of like having no credit score--you're seen as a risk because you have no prior tract record.

Lapse In Insurance


Insurer like dirvers who have history of claim-free driving. If you had a lapse in insurance, your new insurer will take a closer look at why that happended

Factors that affect you premium

Before you start looking at insurance premiums it's importatnt to understand what kinds of factors will affect your premiums. This will help you get a better sense of why your premiums look a certain way. Common factors affecting premium include.

Vehicle Type

Certain cars are considered safer than others because of a variety of factors from make and model to age and even size.

Drivers Age

Young drivers (teenagers through young adults aged 25) don't need to do anything wrong to be considered higher risk than older drivers

Drivers Auto Insurance Experiecne

Your insurance experience is like your credit score, in that the longer history you have, the easier it is for your insurance company to assess how risky you are

Postal Code

Most traffic accidents occur close to home, so if you live in a densely populated area with high traffic, you're going to have a higher rate(plus, urban areas have higher rates of theft and vandalism and generally charge more for car repairs)

Driving Behavior

This one is pretty straigh forward: if you engage in risky driving behaviors (traffic violations, at-fault accidents, DUIs, etc) you,re going to have higher premiums because you,re riskier to insure
